About Me

I am enrolled in joint PhD program at Freie Universität Berlin and Continental Automotive, supervised by Prof. Adrian Paschke. I am specializing in the intersection of artificial intelligence and autonomous systems.

My research is dedicated to developing innovative methods for formalizing traffic rules and integrating them into autonomous driving systems. Additionally, I work as a research scientist at Continental Automotive, where I focus on translating these theoretical frameworks into practical applications.

With a background in both mathematics and computer science, I combine formal methods with modern AI approaches to create safer, more reliable autonomous systems that can understand and follow human-defined rules while navigating complex environments.

Current Research

Trajectory Prediction with Rules and Uncertainty

Working towards integrating traffic rules into the trajectory prediction module in an implicit manner. Alongside we are improving uncertainty quantification in the prediction.

  • Rule-aware prediction models
  • Uncertainty quantification
  • Neuro-symbolic integration
Trajectory Planning with Rule Integration

Algorithms for planning vehicle trajectories in complex traffic scenarios while adhering to the rules of the road.

  • Formal verification of plans
  • Multi-agent coordination
  • Safety-critical scenarios
